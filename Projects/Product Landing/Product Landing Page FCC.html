To Pimp a Butterfly

Album Art

Shot by French photographer Denis Rouvre, the album cover of To Pimp A Butterfly is an epitome of what lies in the next 78 minutes: a black revolution on the White House lawn.
According to the rapper himself, the album cover represents "a group of the homies who haven't seen the world and putting them in these places that they haven't necessarily seen, or only on TV and showing them something different other than the neighborhood and them being excited about it. That's why they have them wild faces on there."

Voice of the People

There were chants from Kendrick Lamar’s ‘Alright’ and of ‘hands up, don’t shoot’, addressing hope for change and a serious frustration as a result of the shocking police brutality in America and oppression worldwide. The atmosphere was amazing, very powerful in a positive way, especially seeing the care amongst the youth here.”
- ZARA JOAN MILLER
